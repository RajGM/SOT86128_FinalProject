# Climate Diplomacy — v1 Game Mechanics

## Overview

11 building types. 4 raw materials. 8 countries. Shared global temperature.
Every building needs workers. Every choice has a tradeoff.

---

## Raw Materials & Extraction

### How It Works

1. **Deposit tiles exist on the hex map** — each country has a different distribution of fuel, uranium, and rare_earth tiles. This is fixed at game start based on geography.

2. **Build an Extractor on a deposit tile** — Extractors can ONLY be placed on tiles that have a deposit. No deposit = can't build extractor. No research unlock required.

3. **Extractor produces that specific material each cycle** — A fuel-tile extractor produces fuel. A uranium-tile extractor produces uranium. Automatic, no ambiguity.

4. **Use it or trade it** — Materials go into your national stockpile. You decide:
   - **Use domestically:** Fuel powers Fossil Plants. Uranium powers Nuclear Plants. Rare earth boosts Manufacturing output.
   - **Trade with other countries:** Sell your surplus for money, food, tech, or other materials you lack.

### Extractor Building

| | T1 | T2 | T3 |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Build cost** | 70 money | +55 | +70 |
| **Workforce** | 5 | 5 | 5 |
| **Recurring input** | 3 money/cycle | 5 money/cycle | 8 money/cycle |
| **Yield/cycle** | 2 units of tile material | 4 units | 7 units |
| **CO₂/cycle** | +3 | +4 | +5 |
| **Terrain** | Deposit tile only (fuel, uranium, or rare_earth) |

Simple: more tier = more extraction. Extraction always produces some CO₂ (mining, drilling, processing).

### Material Distribution by Country

| Country | Fuel tiles | Uranium tiles | Rare Earth tiles | Extraction profile |
|---------|-----------|--------------|-----------------|-------------------|
| USA | Many | Some | Few | Fossil-rich, some nuclear option |
| EU | Few | Few | Few | Resource-poor, must import |
| Russia | Many | Some | Some | Energy exporter |
| China | Some | Few | Many | Rare earth monopoly |
| India | Few | Few | Few | Resource-poor, must import |
| OPEC | Very many | None | None | All fossil, nothing else |
| LatAm | Few | Some | Some | Modest, balanced |
| Africa | Some | Some | Many | Mineral-rich, undeveloped |

This distribution drives the entire trade game:
- OPEC has fuel but nothing else → must sell fuel to survive
- China has rare earth → leverage over manufacturing/tech
- EU has almost nothing → must trade for everything
- Africa has minerals but no money to extract them

## How Materials Connect to Buildings

### Fuel → Fossil Plant

Fossil Plants consume fuel to operate. No fuel = plant goes idle.

| Fossil Plant Tier | Fuel consumed/cycle |
|-------------------|-------------------|
| T1 | 1 fuel |
| T2 | 2 fuel |
| T3 | 3 fuel |

A country with no fuel deposits must either:
- Import fuel via trade (creates dependency — real-world parallel)
- Skip fossil and go green (expensive, needs tech)

### Uranium → Nuclear Plant

Nuclear Plants consume uranium to operate. No uranium = plant goes idle.

| Nuclear Plant Tier | Uranium consumed/cycle |
|-------------------|----------------------|
| T1 | 1 uranium |
| T2 | 1 uranium |
| T3 | 2 uranium |

Uranium is scarce. Only a few countries have it. This makes nuclear a geopolitical resource — countries with uranium can gatekeep clean energy access.

### Rare Earth → Manufacturing (bonus)

Manufacturing works without rare earth, but having it gives a production bonus.

| Manufacturing Tier | Without rare earth | With rare earth (1+/cycle) |
|-------------------|-------------------|---------------------------|
| T1 | +20 goods, +3 tech | +30 goods, +4 tech |
| T2 | +40 goods, +5 tech | +60 goods, +7 tech |
| T3 | +65 goods, +8 tech | +95 goods, +12 tech |

Rare earth isn't consumed — having a stockpile of 1+ activates the bonus. This models the real-world rare earth advantage (China controls ~60% of global supply).

## Workforce System

**Every building has a fixed workforce number.** Sum all your buildings' workforce = total labor demand.

**If population < total labor demand:** newest-built buildings go idle first (LIFO). Idle buildings produce nothing and consume nothing. They reactivate when population recovers.

**Population is tradeable:** countries can trade population (immigration/labor deals). India sends 50 workers to EU in exchange for 20 technology. India loses workforce, EU gains it. Both sides take a risk.

### Workforce Budget Example

| Building | Workers |
|----------|---------|
| 2× Fossil Plant | 20 |
| 1× Industrial Complex | 20 |
| 1× Farm | 5 |
| 1× City | 10 |
| 1× Extractor | 5 |
| **Total demand** | **60** |

If your population is 50, the most recently built building goes idle until you grow or import workers.

## Technology — How It Actually Works

Technology is **engineering capacity**, not "research." Think of it as: "does your country have enough know-how to build a solar farm or a nuclear reactor?"

### The loop

1. **Manufacturing generates it** — the only building that produces tech (+3/+5/+8 per cycle by tier). Rare earth bonus pushes this higher.
2. **Green and Nuclear plants spend it** — building a T1 Green Plant costs 120 money AND 10 tech. Both are deducted from your stockpile. Tech is consumed like money, not a permanent unlock.
3. **Tradeable** — countries with surplus tech can sell it. "I'll give you 15 tech for 30 food" is a valid deal.

### Why it matters

| Country | Starting tech | Has Manufacturing? | Situation |
|---------|--------------|-------------------|-----------|
| EU | 85 | Can build | Self-sufficient. Can sell tech to others. |
| USA | 75 | Can build | Similar to EU. Tech exporter. |
| China | 70 | Can build + rare earth bonus | Tech powerhouse. Leverage over everyone. |
| Russia | 55 | Can build | Moderate. Enough for own green transition. |
| India | 45 | Must build or import | Can afford T1 green (costs 10) but runs out fast without Manufacturing. |
| OPEC | 40 | Must build or import | Same as India. Fossil lock-in partly because they can't afford green. |
| LatAm | 38 | Must build or import | Needs tech imports for any clean energy. |
| Africa | 30 | Must build or import | Lowest tech. Completely dependent on imports for green transition. |

A Green Plant costs 10 tech at T1, 18 cumulative at T2, 23 at T3. Africa starts with 30 — enough for exactly 3 T1 green plants and then they're out. Without Manufacturing or a trade deal, they're stuck on fossil forever.

**This is the real-world dynamic:** developing countries can't transition without technology transfer from industrialized nations. The game makes this a concrete resource constraint, not just a talking point.

### No separate research system

The old design had research levels, extraction unlocks, breakthroughs, combined projects, and research licenses. All cut. Technology from Manufacturing is enough. One source, one use, one trade item. Simple.

## Cycle Processing Order

Each cycle:

1. **Workforce check** — calculate total labor demand vs population. Idle newest buildings if short (LIFO).
2. **Extraction** — active extractors produce materials (fuel/uranium/rare_earth) into stockpile.
3. **Material consumption** — fossil plants consume fuel, nuclear consumes uranium. If stockpile empty → plant goes idle this cycle.
4. **Building yields** — active buildings produce their outputs (energy, food, money, goods, tech, population, happiness).
5. **Building recurring costs** — active buildings consume their inputs (energy, food, money).
6. **Trade processing** — continuous trades transfer resources. Transport emissions applied. Transit fees deducted.
7. **CO₂ → Temperature** — sum all CO₂ from all countries → update global temperature.
8. **Climate effects** — if temp > +2.0°C, farms lose 20% yield. If > +3.0°C, farms lose 40%.
9. **Happiness check** — if food or energy < 0, happiness drops sharply. If happiness < 30, population starts leaving (−5% per cycle).
10. **Advance cycle counter.**

## Key Design Loops

### The fossil dependency loop
Build fossil → need fuel → extract or import → creates CO₂ → temperature rises → farms lose yield → food crisis → unhappiness → population leaves → can't staff buildings → economy collapses.

### The green transition loop
Build manufacturing → generate tech → build green plant → no fuel needed → no CO₂ → farms safe → stable food → happy population → more workers → more manufacturing → more tech.

### The trade dependency loop
EU has no fuel or rare earth → must import from Russia/OPEC/China → creates political dependency → those countries have leverage → can demand concessions → but if they push too hard, EU finds alternatives (green) → fossil exporters lose their market.

### The population leverage loop
India/Africa have huge population but low money/tech → can "export" workers for tech → but losing workers means own buildings go idle → must balance workforce exports vs domestic needs.

## What Changed from Current Implementation

| Before | After | Why |
|--------|-------|-----|
| 5 energy types (coal, oil, gas, solar, hydro) | 3 (fossil, green, nuclear) | Same tradeoff, fewer choices to confuse players |
| Extractor on any tile, research unlock first | Extractor only on deposit tiles, no research needed | Simpler, more intuitive |
| Deposits as parallel economy (separate stock) | Materials integrated as regular tradeable resources | One economy, not two |
| No fuel consumption by plants | Fossil needs fuel, nuclear needs uranium | Creates real resource dependency and trade pressure |
| `mixed` deposit type | Removed | Vague, no clear use |
| 18+ building types | 11 | Every building earns its spot |
| Research system (levels, unlocks, breakthroughs, licenses) | Cut entirely. Tech comes from Manufacturing. | Research was a mini-game inside the game. Manufacturing is enough. |
| Population decorative | Population = workforce, tradeable | Makes population matter mechanically |
