------Layer 1: Scaarcity Tragedy of the Commons
starting with Hardin (1968) + Olson (1965)
Each actor exploits a shared resource for individual gain.
No one bears the full cost of overuse.
Rational self-interest leads to collective ruin.
Free-riders benefit without contributing.

Game Mechanic
Every fossil building produces CO₂ → shared global
temperature.
Temperature rises penalise ALL 8 countries equally.
Unilateral cuts cost you fully, benefit everyone 1/8th.
No global authority enforces cooperation.
------

------Layer 2: Carbon Pricing
Nordhaus DICE Model (2018 Nobel)
Carbon tax as abatement cost instrument.
Pricing emissions shifts investment incentives.
Tax never directly removes CO₂ — it changes
behaviour by making pollution expensive.

Game Mechanic
Each country sets tax rate 0–100.
Collects money proportional to national emissions.
Tax does NOT reduce CO₂ directly.
Players must physically replace fossil → green.
Higher tax = more revenue but economic drag.

-------Layer 3: Revenue Recycling

Goulder (1995) Double Dividend Hypothesis
How you spend carbon tax revenue determines whether the tax helps or hurts the economy.
Meta-analysis of 69 CGE simulations (Freire-González 2018) confirms: recycling method matters more than tax level.
